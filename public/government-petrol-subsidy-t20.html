Government reconsiders petrol subsidy for T20

By: PULSEPOINT NEWS Source: Government advisory Published: 2026

KUALA LUMPUR: The government is reviewing its petrol subsidy framework for the T20 income group amid growing pressure to rebalance public spending and improve long-term energy efficiency.

Officials said an internal assessment will look at whether the current subsidy arrangement is sustainable and whether a more targeted approach could free up funds for infrastructure and green energy programs.

The review follows several weeks of stakeholder consultations with industry representatives, economists and consumer groups. Early feedback suggests that while the T20 group benefits from current subsidy levels, the policy may not be aligned with broader equity goals.

Sources close to the review said the government is considering a phased approach that would maintain support for low-income households while gradually reducing subsidies for higher earners in the T20 band.

Economic analysts note that petrol consumption among the T20 has remained relatively stable, and any changes will need to be carefully communicated to avoid sudden shifts in market expectations.

The proposed adjustment is not intended to disrupt daily mobility, but rather to preserve subsidies for targeted groups and improve the effectiveness of the national fuel support system.

As the matter develops, ministers have emphasized that no final decision has been made. The review is expected to continue through the next quarter, with an interim report due to be released after further consultation.

In the meantime, the government continues to monitor oil price movements and regional energy trends to ensure that any changes are introduced in a way that supports both economic stability and social fairness.
